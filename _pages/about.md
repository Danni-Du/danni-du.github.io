I am Danni Du (杜旦尼), a postdoctoral research associate in the Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences (AOS) Program at Princeton University, affiliated with NOAA GFDL. I am advised by Dr. Alistair Adcroft and Dr. Feiyu Lu, as part of the [M²LInES](https://m2lines.github.io/) project. I received my Ph.D. degree from the Department of Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences (ATOC) at University of Colorado Boulder in August 2024, where I was advised by Dr. Aneesh Subramanian and Dr. Weiqing Han. 

My research aims to advance weather and climate prediction through three complementary pathways: 1) understanding the predictability at the subseasonal-to-seasonal (S2S) time scales; 2) improving process-based physical understanding, such as air-sea interactions; 3) combining machine learning with traditional modeling to systematically correct prediction biases.
